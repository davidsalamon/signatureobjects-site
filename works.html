Zante Sconce

Edition: Limited series

Designer: Zsófi Zala

The shape of the Zante wall lamp was inspired by the receding waters of the ocean during Zsófi's stay in Portugal, and the name comes from the Portuguese word vazante, tide.

Zsófi used the introspective, soft form to create a sense of the way light is created. The patinated copper plate highlights the light that shines from within the form, while the contrasting touch and shade of the American walnut that makes up the rest of the body enhances the contrast between light and dark.

Wind Light

Edition: 100

Designer: David Salamon

Wind Light is a one-of-a-kind designer lamp. The poetic luminaire is produced in a limited edition, where each example is unique.

The shape of Wind Light is inspired by the forces of nature: wind, gravity and the interaction between molecules of matter. It is the physical embodiment of an intangible, digital sensation, achieved through computer simulation and 3d printing.

In designing it, David was looking for an answer to the question: how might we leverage the characteristics of 3D printing to our advantage in a collectible design object?

Cnidaria Table Lamp

Edition: Limited series

Designer: Andras Kerekgyarto

CNIDARIA reinterprets the mushroom lamp as a fluid, continuous form, realised in a single piece of blown glass. Its inverted toroidal geometry dissolves the distinction between base and shade, while its soft internal glow evokes a living presence — closer to a drifting organism than a conventional object.

The piece is as much a study in form as it is in light. Its tilted posture and seamless volume required over two years of development in close collaboration with a master glassblower, pushing the limits of balance, control, and material behaviour. CNIDARIA stands as a crafted exercise in precision and intuition — where technique enables the illusion of effortlessness.

Fear and Uncertainty

Edition: Limited series

Designer: David Salamon

This limited edition series reflects on the past years since the COVID-19 outbreak: an era when fear and uncertainty gripped a generation worldwide.

In today’s societies, uncertainty seems to take over. It has become increasingly difficult to distinguish right from wrong, and even the role of science and medicine is being questioned.

This diptych embodies that ideological divide—two wall sconces standing in stark contrast, reflecting a world increasingly seen in black and white. Their elongated forms evoke a near-sacral presence, nodding to the almost messianic hope once placed in vaccines.

Designers

Zsofi Zala

Zsófi Zala’s work is guided by a search for harmony; creating objects that resonate both within and around us. Rooted in traditional craftsmanship and a sensitive use of natural materials, her practice is defined by precision, tactility, and an open, exploratory mindset.

A graduate of Moholy-Nagy University of Art and Design, Zsófi moves fluidly between furniture, lighting, and large-scale installations, while also teaching design. Her pieces often carry personal narratives, where form and material come together to evoke a quiet sense of balance and calm.

Blanka Timári and Balázs Kisgyörgy

Balázs Kisgyörgy and Blanka Timári operate across objects, installations, and bespoke commissions; from small-scale props to large spatial works. Their practice is defined by versatility and making: each project demands a different solution, and their strength lies in adapting their tools and thinking to the task at hand.

Both trained as product designers at MOME, they bring a strong foundation in design and craftsmanship to their work. Alongside commissioned projects, they develop their own objects as a parallel practice, driven by curiosity, hands-on experimentation, and a desire to create something direct, engaging, and precise.
